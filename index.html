The Journey

How It Works

A simple five-step process to help young people access the support they need and transition towards independent living.

1

Referral or Enquiry

Get in touch with us directly or through your social worker, support worker, or local authority. We'll discuss your needs and how we can help.

2

Assessment & Support Planning

We conduct a comprehensive assessment to understand your needs, goals, and preferences. Together, we create a personalised support plan.

3

Placement in Supported Accommodation

Move into safe, comfortable accommodation where you'll have your own space and access to 24/7 support when needed.

4

Regular Support Meetings & Life Skills Coaching

Work with your dedicated support worker through regular meetings, skills workshops, and personalised coaching sessions.

5

Transition Towards Independence

When you're ready, we support your move to independent living with ongoing guidance and aftercare to ensure long-term success.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can access VISHY services?

VISHY supports young people aged 16–24, particularly care leavers, those at risk of homelessness, and young people transitioning from residential care or family breakdown. We work with local authorities, youth services, and social care teams to provide placements for those who need supported accommodation.

What does supported accommodation provide?

Our supported accommodation provides: a fully furnished private room, access to shared living spaces (kitchen, bathroom, lounge), 24/7 support availability, regular one-to-one support sessions, life skills training, help with education and employment, and a pathway to independent living. All our properties are in safe, accessible locations.

How do I apply or get referred?

You can access our services through several routes: local authority referral, social worker referral, youth service referral, or self-referral by contacting us directly. We accept referrals from all local authorities and partner organisations. Simply get in touch via our contact form or phone, and we'll guide you through the process.

What support can I expect?

Every young person receives personalised support including: a dedicated keyworker, weekly support meetings (minimum), help with practical skills like cooking and budgeting, emotional wellbeing support, education and employment guidance, help accessing healthcare and other services, support with housing applications for independent living, and aftercare support even after you've moved on.

How long can I stay in supported accommodation?

Placement length varies depending on individual needs and local authority arrangements. Typically, young people stay between 6 months and 2 years. Our focus is on supporting you to develop the skills and confidence you need for independent living. We'll work with you to create a transition plan and prepare you for your next steps.
